01 — What I See

The Recognition Is There.
The Commercial Path Can Be Clearer.

From the outside, I see a brand with real recognition, but a commercial story that could be easier for buyers to follow.

01 —
People understand KissCam quickly
The moment is immediately familiar. That makes the sales conversation easier to start. The opportunity is not to over-explain the moment. It is to make the business offer around it clearer for buyers who are ready to act.
02 —
Each buyer type needs its own path
A venue director, sponsor manager and licensing partner all have different questions and different approval processes. Right now they land in roughly the same place. Giving each one a direct lane removes real friction from the sales process.
03 —
That is where I think I can help
I help build the commercial web presence, SEO structure, inquiry paths and outreach assets that turn interest into real conversations. That is the work I am proposing. Not a generic website redesign. Commercial packaging, buyer paths and growth infrastructure.

02 — Where I Think I Can Help

Commercial Packaging
and Growth Infrastructure.

I am not approaching this as a website polish project. I am looking at it as a commercial packaging and growth opportunity.

■
A clearer commercial homepage
A homepage that tells the right buyer, in the first few seconds, what KissCam offers, who it helps and what to do next. Page hierarchy, headline clarity and a cleaner call to action make a measurable difference in how buyers respond.
■
Separate paths for each buyer
Venue teams, sponsors, licensing partners and media buyers all have different questions. Separate landing pages let KissCam answer each audience directly instead of asking everyone to sort it out themselves.
■
SEO and content foundation
Page structure, metadata, internal linking, schema cleanup and a simple blog strategy built around commercial search intent. The goal is to turn existing brand attention into inbound conversations instead of traffic that disappears.
■
Tracking and inquiry flow
Clean contact forms, GA4 and GTM setup, lead routing and conversion paths so interest does not disappear into a generic inbox. Know where leads come from, what they looked at and how to follow up.
■
Outreach and pitch assets
Simple one-sheets, sponsor-facing pages, partner emails and landing pages that help start real conversations with venues, sponsors and media partners. Content that works before a sales call and after one.
A focused first sprint could cover
Getting the commercial foundation right
  • Clean partner messaging and page hierarchy
  • Venue, sponsor and licensing paths
  • SEO and content structure
  • GA4 and GTM tracking setup
  • Inquiry flow and lead routing
  • Sponsor-ready one-sheets and outreach assets
03 — Proof Worth Packaging

The Pieces Are Already
on the Board.

From what I observed publicly, KissCam already has real commercial activity behind it. What I think is missing is a way to communicate that proof clearly to new partners before the first conversation even starts.

Signal 01
Entertainment Licensing Activity
Publicly visible signals point to real licensing conversations and media relationships in motion. That kind of activity is a credibility signal for any new partner who is researching KissCam before agreeing to a first call. It should be visible and easy to find.
Signal 02
Consumer Product Collaboration
A co-branded consumer product demonstrates brand reach beyond the arena. It signals the ability to extend KissCam's identity into retail and lifestyle categories — which is exactly the kind of proof licensing partners and brand sponsors want to see before moving forward.
Signal 03
Technology and Fan Engagement Assets
Patented technology and proprietary fan engagement tools give commercial partners something concrete to license or deploy. That is more than a recognizable name. It is a platform with real assets behind it — and that distinction matters in a partner conversation.
Signal 04
A Cultural Moment Brands Already Understand
The underlying asset is a crowd moment that audiences have recognized and responded to for decades. That kind of familiarity does not need to be invented or explained. It needs a commercial frame that lets the right brand partners act on it quickly and confidently.
I would want to verify current status and scope with you directly before using any of this in formal partner materials. The goal is to build a version of the commercial story that is both accurate and compelling.
04 — The Market Is Already Moving

Who Is Already
In This Space.

A few well-resourced companies are actively selling fan engagement technology to venues and sponsors right now. Most of them are built around infrastructure and data. What KissCam has is a moment people already know by name — before the platform is even introduced. That is a different kind of commercial opening, and it is worth framing clearly.

Companies competing for the same venue and sponsor budget
Fan Engagement Platforms
CUE Uplause Monterosa Fan of the Match
Well-funded and actively pitching venues and sponsors. Strong on data, reporting and platform technology. Built around infrastructure and analytics — not around a named crowd moment that audiences already recognize before the product is explained.
Stadium AR and Live Event Tech
ARound by Stagwell BrandXR Rock Paper Reality
Deployed in major league stadiums and competing for venue entertainment budgets. Serious technology infrastructure and real venue relationships. Built for technical execution and visual spectacle — the emotional territory around a shared crowd moment is a different conversation entirely.
Who KissCam should be selling to
Romance and Relationship Brands
Brands like Bumble, Hinge, Zola and Match spend real money to be associated with romantic public moments. KissCam creates those moments. They are not competitors. They are target sponsors.
Jewelry, Flowers and Gift Categories
Valentine's, proposals, anniversaries and date nights are established sponsorship categories. KissCam has a natural connection to all of them. The sponsorship idea is easy to understand when the offer is packaged clearly.
Sports and Venue Sponsors Looking for Moments
Beer, insurance, finance and automotive brands sponsor live sports specifically to be present during high-attention crowd moments. KissCam is associated with one of the most reliably positive of those moments.
The infrastructure competitors are serious and well-deployed. What KissCam brings to that same buyer conversation is a moment audiences already understand and respond to — before the pitch even starts. The opportunity is connecting that recognition to a cleaner partner story before the window closes.
05 — Mockup Directions

What a Partner-Ready
KissCam Could Look Like.

These are not design themes. Each concept shows how KissCam could be presented to a different commercial buyer with a clearer purpose and a clearer next step.

For: All buyers — commercial front door
Homepage Concept
The commercial front door for KissCam. Orients every buyer type in the first scroll: venue teams, sponsors, licensing partners and media buyers each see a path forward. Positions KissCam as a partner platform, not just a live-event feature.
For: Media partners and licensing conversations
Licensing and Media Page
Built for media partners, brand extension conversations and IP licensing inquiries. Explains what can be explored: content partnerships, brand extensions and commercial licensing paths. Gives a licensing director or media buyer a clear picture before agreeing to a first call.
For: Venue entertainment and operations teams
Venue Partner Page
Built for the person who has to pitch KissCam internally before it gets on the calendar. Covers what KissCam adds to the event, how it fits into the existing show, how sponsors can be included and what the next step looks like. Removes the guesswork for the director making the case upstairs.
For: Brand sponsors and partnership managers
Sponsor Page
A sponsor sales page, not a platform overview. Covers what sponsors get, where the brand appears, event-day visibility, social and content opportunities and how to start the conversation. Built for the partnership manager who needs to explain the investment to someone above them. Relevant categories: dating apps, jewelry, restaurants, florists, travel and local date-night brands.
